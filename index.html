About MaghSec

Maghreb Security, operating the MaghSec platform, is a pioneering cybersecurity initiative based in Morocco, serving the entire North African region. Our mission is to bridge the gap between organizations seeking to secure their digital assets and the talented security researchers across the Maghreb.

In today's interconnected world, cybersecurity threats are constantly evolving. Traditional security measures alone are insufficient. Our platform facilitates responsible vulnerability disclosure, allowing companies to proactively identify and fix security weaknesses before they can be exploited by malicious actors.

We adhere to international best practices in vulnerability disclosure while respecting local regulations and business cultures across Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia, Libya, and Mauritania. Our platform ensures clear communication, fair compensation for researchers, and responsible handling of all reported vulnerabilities.

For Security Researchers

Rules & Guidelines

  • Only test systems that are explicitly in scope
  • Do not access or exfiltrate any data
  • Avoid disrupting services (no DoS/DDoS testing)
  • Report vulnerabilities as soon as they're found
  • Do not disclose vulnerabilities publicly before they're fixed
  • Follow responsible disclosure timelines
  • Respect privacy and confidentiality

Required Skills

  • Web application security testing
  • Network penetration testing
  • Understanding of OWASP Top 10
  • Familiarity with common vulnerabilities
  • Ability to write clear vulnerability reports
  • Knowledge of responsible disclosure practices

How to Submit Reports

  • Use the form in the 'Submit Report' section
  • Provide clear reproduction steps
  • Include proof of concept (screenshots, videos)
  • Specify impact and severity
  • Wait for our response before taking further action
  • We aim to respond within 7 business days

Responsible Disclosure Policy

MaghSec Responsible Disclosure Policy

1. Authorization Requirement
All security testing must be explicitly authorized by the target organization through the MaghSec platform. Testing systems without proper authorization is strictly prohibited and may constitute illegal activity.

2. Scope Limitation
Researchers must only test systems and applications that are explicitly listed in the target's scope. Testing outside the defined scope is not permitted.

8. No Illegal Activity
MaghSec does not condone or support any illegal activities. All participants must comply with applicable laws and regulations in Morocco and the relevant jurisdictions.

This policy is subject to change. Last updated: January 2026
